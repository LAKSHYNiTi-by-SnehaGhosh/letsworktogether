# Vision

## The Ultimate Destination
To become the global standard for proving professional capability, replacing traditional resumes and entry-level technical interviews with verifiable execution history from simulated AI workplaces.

## The Emotional Target
When a user logs into LWT, they should not feel like they are "learning." They should feel a sense of:
- **Responsibility**: "My team is waiting for this feature."
- **Ownership**: "I own this architecture."
- **Professionalism**: "I need to communicate clearly in my standup."
- **Accountability**: "If I miss my deadline, it affects the sprint."

## Design & Experience Vision
- **Aesthetics**: LWT must rival the best enterprise software in the world (Linear, Notion, Vercel, Slack). It must be visually stunning, perfectly spaced, and feel premium.
- **The AI Illusion**: The AI must never feel like a language model generating text. It must feel like distinct, capable, and sometimes demanding colleagues. The CEO should care about business metrics; the Tech Lead should be strict about SOLID principles.
- **Dynamic Interface**: Micro-interactions, hover states, fluid transitions, and a flawless dark mode create a living, breathing workspace.
